The Base

The survivors must remain close to the base to survive the cold of night.

Typically, each room in the base is one zone.

Zone Features

Burning

When a round ends, each player in a burning zone must succeed on a Finesse Roll or suffer Burned (Level-1 Harm).

Cluttered

Cramped

Dark

With night comes encompassing darkness. Any Grit, Finesse, and Sense rolls made by characters without a light source and that are not blind get -3d.

Freezing

The air on this world freezes at night. When a round ends, each player in a freezing zone who is not wearing insulated gear must succeed on a Grit Roll or suffer Hypothermia (Level-1 Harm).

Unfiltered

Most outdoor zones will have this feature. When a round ends, each player in a unfiltered zone who is not wearing a mask filter must succeed on a Grit Roll or suffer Toxic Air (Level-1 Harm).

That Which Lurks in the Dark

Creatures are drawn to areas with heat and light.

Preparation Phase

While the sky is yet orange, once all survivors are returned to the base, this is the time to prepare.

Fuel

In order to provide heat, light, and air, the generator must be fueled. The generator consumes fuel depending on how much power is being used- in general, 1 Supply can power one room for 4 hours. Power can be specifically rerouted to and from rooms to conserve power or to alter the battlefield; this can be done during any phase.

Any room without power is dark, freezing, and unfiltered. While the generator is empty, all rooms lose power.

Traps

Spend 1 supply to coat a zone with fuel. Disable sprinklers, light as an action, roll each turn it burns.

Hidden Phase

This phase ends when a survivor is seen.

Every round in the Hidden Phase is equivalent to 6 rounds in the Holdout Phase.

Whenever moving from one zone to another, roll Finesse. -Effect in cluttered zones.

Holdout Phase

Round Structure

Each round progresses in the following order:

  1. Top of Round. Each player chooses "Fast Turn" or "Slow Turn".
  2. Player Fast Turns. Each player who chose "Fast Turn" may take one action. Players take turns simultaneously, but once a player has begun an action, they must resolve that action before any player may take another action.
  3. Enemy Fast Turns. Each enemy taking a fast turn acts.
  4. Player Slow Turns. Each player did not act during fast turns may take two actions. Again, players take turns simultaneously, but once a player has begun an action, they must resolve that action before any player may take another action.
  5. Enemy Slow Turns. Each enemy which did not take a fast turn acts.
  6. End of Round.

Skills

Stress Track Modifying Skills

Physique - add a box to the Health stress track per skill level. Corresponds to physical well-being, and is checked when exposed to disease and moving heavy objects

Resolve - add a box to the Composure stress track per skill level. Corresponds to dedication to objectives, and is used to defend against mental attacks

Assets - add a box to the Finance stress track per skill level. Corresponds to wealth and making purchases

Combat Skills

Brawling - fist, feet, and found weapons

Melee - knives, swords, spears, energy swords, etc

Firearms - whether black powder, cordite, obscure binary propellants, or any other form of slug-throwing

Energy Weapons - lasers, plasma weapons, lightning guns, or anything else that does harm with energy

Psionics - psychic abilities. a character may not have more psychic stunts than levels in this skill (ie a +2 Psionics may have 0. 1. or 2 psychic stunts)

Professional Skills

Brokerage - knowledge of interstellar trade and how to manipulate it

Bureaucracy - facility with handling the people and paperwork associated with government and other institutional processes

Biology - recognize living things and identify their properties, as well as make conclusions about and handle newly discover flora and fauna

Computer - evaluate data, reprogram a device, hack, subvert, destroy, or otherwise manipulate data and data carrying systems

Demolitions - the use of explosives

Driving - operate terrestrial vehicles on land or water

Engineering - keep the ship in space, moving, and at a reasonable temperature; repair damage to mechanical and electronic systems, excluding software and primitive devices

Medical - low levels reflect basic first aid; advanced levels reflect the skills of a professional surgeon or internist.

Navigation - locating that envelope of space where the slipstream can be entered is tricky business, and the Navigator is the one who knows it inside out

Piloting - used to influence each turn’s positioning roll. It also might be used outside of that system to resolve an escape scene quickly or to conduct a complicated orbital maneuver

Profession: <choice> - players choose a profession and can expect to perform any tasks related to that profession using this Skill rank. Examples: Bartender, Butler, Farmer, Naval architect, Blacksmith, Teacher. May be taken more than once

Tactics - pick out key details to use to make advantage

Physical Skills

Agility - how fast, flexible, and dexterous a character is. This is added to defense rolls

Athletics - general level of physical fitness; jumping, running, climbing, swimming, etc

EVA - the facility to move and fight in a very low gravity environment or in life-support equipment

Stealth - sneaking around, and avoiding notice

Survival - survive in the wilderness: building fires, making shelters, surviving hostile environments, etc

Mental Skills

Arts - understanding of the literature, history, and fine arts on all worlds for which the character has C/T

Culture/Tech - represents the facility of the character with culture and technology of a given system in the cluster, as well as some general history. Instead of a world, players may also choose C/T proficiency in "archaic weaponry" to increase weapon proficiencies. See table above

Skill Effect
+0 (untrained) you can get by on your home world.
+1 you can get by comfortably on your homeworld and 1 additional world in the cluster (specify)
+2 you can get by comfortably on your homeworld and 2 additional world in the cluster (specify)
+3 you can get by comfortably on your homeworld and 3 additional world in the cluster (specify)

Investigate - find things out through concentrated effort and in-depth scrutiny

Notice - a character’s surface observation, ability to pick out details, and situational alertness. Determines turn order.

Religion - understanding of the beliefs and rituals on all worlds you have C/T

Science - an understanding of the principles of physics, mathematics, chemistry, and biology

Social Skills

Charm - when you need to be persuasive, you need to speak to them with that honeyed voice and careful elocution that makes them want to love you

Contacts - knowing, making connections with, or influencing the reputation of people

Decieve - lying and misdirection

Empathy - knowing and being able to spot changes in a person’s mood or bearing

Languages - works like C/T, with the addition that characters will be familiar with the common language

Provoke - getting someone’s dander up and eliciting negative a emotional response from them—fear, anger, shame, etc. It’s the “being a jerk” skill

Rapport - positive connections to people, your reputation in the area, and how much you're respected

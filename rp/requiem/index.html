The Masquerade and the Requiem

Crowning the northern tip of Akoros, past the deathlands and only accessable by train, stands tall the black city Duskwall. Goat-drawn carriages wind through narrow cobblestone streets under tottering houses built atop apartments built on yet older ruins. Newly installed electric lamps light up expensive state buildings occupied by officials dispassionately cordoning land they've never seen. Great ships depart from the docks to the icy northern depths in search of whales and the oil they produce.

And in the dark corners, in the black canals that snake through blocks to the ocean, in the howling coal mines long derelict, in the mansions of nobles long ago hanged, carries on the masquerade- the smooth monsters wearing damnation like a stylish coat. Immortal intrigue in grim hierarchies, predator-and-prey posturing, and the complex social stratum of bloodsuckers.

It's a downward spiral. It’s a great tragedy that ends in monstrosity and destruction. For some, it’s a constant struggle to maintain ties and never lose sight. Most Kindred find a range of Humanity that suits them, a certain distance or closeness to the breathing world.

What are you going to do to make it through tonight? What about tomorrow night?

After the deeds are done and your belly’s full, what are you going to do with your damnation that makes it worth all the sins along the way?

So, too, carries on the requiem.

The Game

REQUIEM IN DUSKWALL is a game about a group of young vampires fighting tooth and nail for their place in a cramped victorian city. You are a monster so close to being human. You are a coyote afraid of all the bigger predators.

In the All Night Society, the neonates bear the dread weight of all above them. They are the numerous, the expendable. The ancillae influence you, and so do the elders through them. Gravity is the unfair tyrant; blood flows up and shit flows down. If you could just survive another night...

Conditions

The list below includes common Conditions that can be applied to characters. For Skill-based rolls, an exceptional success allows the noted Condition (unless otherwise stated). It may go to your character, or to the subject of the roll, as noted.

Temporary conditions last until the resolution is met, at which point the character clears the condition and takes a beat. Temporary conditions will also fade away when appropriate, usually after a scene, but the character does not take a beat this way. Persistent conditions require greater effort to end depending on the specifics of the situation. They can still be resolved once per chapter to take a beat, but this will usually not end the condition.

Temporary Conditions

Blind

Your character cannot see. Any rolls requiring sight may only use a chance die. If another sense can be reasonably substituted, make the roll at -3 instead.

This Condition may be taken as Persistent.

Resolution: Limitation or difficulty that heightens immediate danger.

Bonded

Your character has established an extensive bond with an animal. They gain +2 on any rolls to influence or persuade the animal once per scene. The animal may add your Animal Ken to any rolls to remain loyal in the face of coercion or fear. The animal may add your character’s Animal Ken to any one die roll.

Resolution: The bonded animal dies.

Confused

Your character cannot think straight, either because of some mental power or good old-fashioned cranial trauma. You take a –2 die penalty on all Intelligence and Wits rolls.

Resolution: Take any amount of lethal damage.

Connected

Your character has made inroads with a specified group. While they have this Condition, they get a +2 to all rolls relating to that group. Alternately, they can shed this Condition to gain a one-time +3 bonus to influence or otherwise work with the group.

Resolution: Condition shed, bridge burned.

Deprived

Your character suffers from an addiction, and is unable to focus and contain themself. Remove one die from Stamina, Resolve, and Composure dice pools. This does not influence derived traits; it simply influences dice pools that use these Attributes.

This Condition may be taken as Persistent.

Resolution: Indulge in the deprived Vice.

Drained

Your character has suffered extensive blood loss. They suffer a –2 die penalty to any physical actions, and rolls to stabilize and survive injuries. As well, after any scene where they exert themself physically, they must make a Stamina roll or fall unconscious for an hour or more. The Drained penalty does not apply to the Stamina roll, but any wound penalties do apply. Taking damage, being fed from, or spending Willpower on a physical roll applies as physical exertion for this Condition.

Resolution: All lethal damage healed through normal means

Guilty

Your character is experiencing deep-seated feelings of guilt and remorse. This Condition is commonly applied after a successful breaking point roll. While the character is under the effects of this Condition, they receive a -2 to any Resolve or Composure rolls to defend against Subterfuge, Empathy, or Intimidation rolls.

Resolution: The character makes restitution for whatever was done; the character confesses their crimes.

Inspired

Your character is deeply inspired. When your character takes an action pertaining to that inspiration, you may resolve this Condition. Gain a Willpower point, and consider the roll an Exceptional Success on three successes, instead of five.

Resolution: Use the Condition for its benefit.

Mesmerized

Your character’s will is subordinate to that of a greater power. You’re not obviously hypnotized — you’re a bit quiet and reserved compared to normal, but nothing out of the ordinary. When the power who inflicted this Condition gives you a command, you cannot resist. If it’s something that you wouldn’t normally do, you might look like you’ve been hypnotized or that you’re sleepwalking, but otherwise you look and act normally. If you resolve this Condition, gain a +3 die bonus to resist further attempts to Mesmerize you in the same scene; you also can’t quite remember what happened while you were under the spell.

Resolution: Take more bashing or lethal damage than your Stamina. Experience a breaking point when following the command, and succeed at the related Resolve + Composure roll. Follow the command.

Sated

Your character gave their vice an outlet. Until they resolve this Condition, they have a +1 die modifier to rolls to resist their urges.

Resolution: Succumb to vice, or resist a significant provocation.

Scarred

Your character was subject to a violent event and left disturbed, angry, paranoid, and prone to lashing out. With this Condition, take a –2 die penalty to any rolls to resist fear. As well, any creature exhibiting a predatory aura attempting to frighten or intimidate your character receives a +2 die bonus.

Resolution: Lash out physically, causing three or more levels of lethal damage to someone.

Shaken

Something has frightened your character extensively.

Resolution: Any time your character is taking an action where that fear might hinder, you may opt to fail the roll (before you’ve made the roll)

Spooked

Your character has seen something supernatural — not overt enough to terrify, but unmistakably otherworldly. How your character responds to this is up to you, but it captivates and eats their attention.

Resolution: Your character’s fear and fascination causes them to do something that hinders the group or complicates things (going off alone to investigate a strange noise, staying up all night researching, running away instead of holding their ground).

Steadfast

Your character is confident and resolved. When you’ve failed a roll, resolve this Condition to instead treat the action as if you’d rolled a single success. If the roll was a chance die, you may resolve this Condition and roll a single die instead.

Resolution: Use the Condition for its benefit.

Swooning

Your character is attracted to someone and is vulnerable where they are concerned. They may have the proverbial butterflies in their stomach, or just be constantly aware of their object of affection. They suffer a -2 to any rolls that would adversely affect the specified character, who also gains +2 on any Social rolls against your character.

Resolution: Do something for their love that puts your character in danger; opt to fail a roll to resist a social action by the object.

Persistent Conditions

Amnesia

Your character is missing a portion of memory. An entire period of their life is just gone. This causes massive difficulties with friends and loved ones.

Resolution: Something problematic arises, such as a forgotten arrest warrant or old enemy.

Charmed

You’ve been charmed by a supernatural force of personality. You don’t want to believe that anything it says is a lie, and you can’t read its true intentions.

Resolution: You divulge a secret or perform a favor for the entity.

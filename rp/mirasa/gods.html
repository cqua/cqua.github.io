BRUTUS, the Horned King

God of nature, beasts, and chaos.

He takes the most after the Godsâ€™ mother Eryth. He is worshiped by druids and hermits, as well as beastmen hoping for redemption. Unlike other Gods, Brutus has no court of his own. Instead, he presides over and protects the forests and great beasts. Some say that the mortal plane is his court, but if this were true, he has ceded most of his land to the mortals. His symbol is three interlocking horns.

ETURNAM, the Reaper

God of change, luck, and the dead.

They are responsible for the changing of states: summer to winter, fortune to misfortune, life to death. Souls pass between realms only with Eturnam permission. Every Spring, Eturnam faces Suthr in brutal combat, returning Winter to its realm, and every Autumn, Eturnam gently escourts Hestia back to the Summercourt. Eturnam is the patron of necromancers and dedicated warlords, but also of gamblers and sailors. Their symbol is the scythe.

HESTIA, the Summerqueen

God of harvest, life, and the celestial.

Hestia is the patron of healers and lawmakers, romantics and poets. She is responsible for the Summer, and her blessings are more potent under a Summer sky. As such, her powers were especially revered during ancient times in the deserts. Her symbol is the sun.

ILLUNE, the Lunar Maiden

God of the moon, travel, and amnesty.

She is the patron of elves, but also travelers. More than anyone else, her followers are known for going to the needy and providing. Her realm, sometimes called the Lunarcourt, is open for travellers to use to get around the realm quickly. Its halls are infinite however, and one can easily be lost forever. Her symbol is the moon.

THE SEER, the One-Eyed Owl

God of sorcery, and knowledge, and secrecy.

The Seer is said to have been the one to grant humans language. She is the patron of wise men, as well as thieves looking to confide in the oldest keeper of secrets. It is said he dwells in an ancient, infinite library which some call the Shadowcourt. Stories tell of lost souls wandering the passages trying to make sense of the infinite knowledge stored within. Her symbol is a pair of eyes, one blinded.

SUTHR, the Winterking

God of frost, song, and battle.

Suthr is the patron of warriors and bards. He is responsible for the Winter, and his blessings are more potent under a Winter sky. As such, he is especially venerated in the north. During the Summer, he dwells in the Wintercourt, a prison of hate and ice. His symbol is a sprig of spruce.

MINOR GODS

In addition to the major pantheon, hundreds of smaller deities are worshipped throughout the world. Some of these include Amaat God of Love, the Robin Prince, the Storm Queen, Warfather Abaddon, the Deep Shadow of Revel, Baphomet of the Feral Moon (also called Goetherah), and Kha the Boundless Flame.

Beasthood

Characters risk beasthood when they begin to be lost in nature. The woods have a way of claiming souls when one encounters the strange, the unsettling, or the unknowable. Being affected by sylvan magic, encountering faeries and dryads, and witnessing the deepest wells of mana slowly transform mortals into something else.

Whenever you gain beasthood, you become frightened for a number of rounds equal to your new beasthood total. If you are already frightened, you instead become stunned for the duration. When your beasthood total equals your Maximum Beasthood, you are overcome. Roll 2d6 and add them on the beasthood table to determine the outcome. This removes the frightened affliction, and afterward your beasthood is decreased by 1d6+WIL (minimum 1).

Once per day, you can remove all beasthood by taking a Wild Mark from the Mark of the Forest table. These are permanent changes that represent the effects of ancient mana on your body. You may roll on the table or choose a mark that fits your situation. When you do this, your Maximum Beasthood increases permanently by your Will (minimum 1).

You can also spend 24 hours in a human or elven city to remove 1 beasthood.

MARKS OF THE FOREST

Strangers Everywhere. You no longer recognize faces; everyone seems to be the same, differentiated only by their voices and clothing.
Sticky Fingers. You have a compulsion to steal. Whenever you see an item you can hold in one hand that’s neither worn nor carried by another creature, you must take it unless you get a success on a Will challenge roll.
Blank Slate. You lose your personality traits and adopt those of the people currently around you. You become dazed for as long as you cannot see or hear a creature other than you.
Liar, Liar. You can’t tell the truth.
Leaves upon leaves. You can’t read or write and can't learn to read or write.
Haste. You are confident, quick to act, and reckless. You can only take fast turns.
Sloth. You question your every action, overthink, and find it difficult to undertake any activity. You cannot take fast turns.
Overkeen Senses. You see and hear everything in your purview. Your senses are overloaded with animals in the distance, the grass in the wind, trees growing. You make Perception challenge rolls with 3 banes.
Staggering Confidence. You become certain of your capabilities, so much so that you make all attack rolls and challenge rolls with 1 boon. However, if you get a failure, you become dazed for 1 minute.
Ancestral Call. You hear the voices of all of your ancestors at once, who transition between Counsel and Derision. After you complete a rest, the spirits offer Counsel, and you make all challenge rolls with a boon. Whenever you fail a challenge roll, roll a d6. If the result is odd, the spirits begin bickering ceaselessly, offer Derision, and you make all challenge rolls with a bane until you complete a rest.
